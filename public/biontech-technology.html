Technology of Biontech Water Ionizer

Structure of Biontech Water Ionizer

BionTech's ionized water is created through a dual process involving a filtering system and an electrolysis system. The incoming water first undergoes filtration to eliminate heavy metals (e.g., lead), bacteria, VOCs, and other impurities. Following purification, the treated water is directed into the electrolysis chamber for ionization. This process results in the generation of either alkaline ionized water or acidic ionized water.

Biontech Alkaline Ion Water Generation Principle

Clean water that has gone through the purification stage is electrolyzed in the electrolyzer of the electrolysis unit, and acidic ions such as chlorine, sulfuric acid, and nitrate ions gather at the anode (+) to create acidic ion water, and alkaline cations are collected at the cathode (-). Calcium, magnesium, sodium, ions, etc. come together to create alkaline ion water.

Water Containing Minerals

Alkaline ionized water is rich in minerals such as calcium, magnesium, sodium and potassium.

Alkaline Water

Alkaline Ionized Water is alkaline water with a pH of 7.5 to 10.0 or lower
